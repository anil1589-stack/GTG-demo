How GA4 + Tag Gateway Works

1

Browser fires gtag.js

Your GA4 snippet loads from Google's CDN (or your gateway domain) and pushes events into dataLayer.

2

Hits routed to Tag Gateway

With transport_url set, all hits travel to your server-side GTM container instead of going directly to Google.

3

Server enriches & forwards

The gateway can enrich hits with server-side data, filter PII, and fan out to GA4, BigQuery, and other destinations.

Key Benefits

🔒

First-Party Data

Cookies set server-side are first-party and not blocked by ITP or browser extensions.

🛡️

Ad Blocker Resilience

Hits proxied through your own domain bypass most ad blockers, recovering lost measurement.

⚡

Real-Time Control

Redact, transform, or enrich any event server-side before it reaches any analytics destination.
